# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Thai One Piece TCG collectors and players in a Discord community. They open packs to feel the thrill of rare pulls, track their collection progress, and manage custom lots for trading. Context is casual mobile and desktop sessions, often on Discord-embedded browsers or direct links, checking pulls during or after real-world pack openings.

## Product Purpose

POD TCG Simulator recreates the excitement of opening real TCG card packs in a digital format. It exists so the community can simulate pulls, preview rarity outcomes, and maintain a personal collection record without spending money. Success is a pack-opening experience that feels thrilling enough to share, with clear collection tracking that keeps collectors coming back.

## Brand Personality

Exciting. Neon. Competitive.

The interface should pulse with the energy of a high-stakes pull, use bold neon color against dark surfaces to create contrast and focus, and feel like it belongs in a competitive gaming arena rather than a casual toy.

## Anti-references

- Generic gacha simulators with flat, template-like interfaces that feel mass-produced and lack personality. No sterile card grids with no atmosphere, no bare-bones probability sliders, no "just a list of cards" collection views.

## Design Principles

1. **Earn every glow** - Visual effects (holo shimmer, neon glow, particles) celebrate genuine rarity and achievement. A Legendary pull should feel different from a Common pull. Decoration without meaning dilutes the experience.

2. **The pull is the product** - The pack-opening moment is the core experience. Everything around it (collection, lots, navigation) exists to support and extend that moment, not to compete with it.

3. **Thai first, never afterthought** - Typography, spacing, and copy are designed for the Thai language. Ligatures, line heights, and font weights are chosen because they read well in Thai, not because they look good in English and happen to be passable in Thai.

4. **Competitive clarity** - Rarity tiers, pull rates, and collection completeness are legible at a glance. The interface rewards mastery: experienced users can scan and act fast, new users can learn the system by looking.

5. **Dark with purpose** - The dark surface exists to make neon accents, card glows, and rarity effects pop. It's not decoration for its own sake; it's contrast infrastructure that makes moments of excitement visible.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG 2.1 AA minimum for all text and interactive elements
- Current Thai audience may include color-blind users; rarity indicators must not rely on color alone (stars, labels, and border patterns provide redundancy)
- Reduced-motion users must still see card reveals and pack openings (crossfade fallbacks, not blank states)
- Touch targets on mobile must meet minimum 44px for the primary actions (open pack, select cards, navigate tabs)
